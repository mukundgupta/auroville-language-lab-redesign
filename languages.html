Courses we offer

Learn Languages with confidence

Auroville Language Lab teaches English, French, Tamil, Sanskrit, Hindi, German, Italian, Spanish, Japanese, Chinese, and many other languages. Our emphasis is on practical communication, not just grammar.

Lessons blend speaking practice with audio, video, and multimedia so learners build listening and conversation skills naturally.

The Mediatheque and reference library are available to support every stage of your progress, from review and practice to deeper language exploration.

Learning resources

The Mediatheque and the reference library

The Mediatheque provides a rich collection of audio, video, and multimedia exercises that reinforce classroom learning.

Use it to practise pronunciation, review vocabulary, or explore language material at your own pace.

The reference library is also available for deeper study of culture and language structure, making it easy to combine guided lessons with independent practice.

Course formats

Learn the way that suits you

Choose between group lessons, private tuition, online sessions, or guided self-study with the Mediatheque.

Group Classes

Small groups for lively conversation, cultural exchange, and shared practice.

Private Classes

One-on-one lessons tailored to your pace and learning preferences.

Online Lessons

Remote classes with an instructor, suitable for busy schedules.

Guided Self-Study

Study independently with Mediatheque support and occasional tutor guidance.

Self-Learning with the Mediatheque

Learn at your own pace using our digital library, audio, video, and language resources.

Tomatis Listening Method

Train your ear with the Alfred Tomatis method for quicker pronunciation and faster language assimilation.

All course formats are supported by our resources and teacher guidance, so you can keep progressing between lessons.

Evening Programs

Special evening sessions for every learner

We launched a new experiment: the Language Lab now stays open from 5pm to 6pm, Monday through Friday. These evening sessions include conversational practice with native speakers, talks, poetry readings, and guided self-study with the Mediatheque.

The program is free of cost to Aurovilians, Newcomers, Volunteers, Guests, and people from around the Auroville area. If you once learned a language but feel a bit rusty, come and revive it.

Everyone is welcome to join, share language, and practise together in a relaxed, supportive evening setting.

How it works

A simple path from first lesson to real use

1. Start with the right level

We assess your current skills and recommend the format that fits your goals.

2. Learn with native content

Lessons combine conversation, audio, and video so you learn how people actually speak.

3. Practice outside class

Use the Mediatheque and library to review vocabulary and sharpen listening skills.

4. Progress steadily

Regular feedback and consistent practice help you build confidence with each lesson.
